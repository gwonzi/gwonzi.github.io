Commander Decklists

Take a peek at our 99

Our decks can be found on MTGGoldfish, but unfortunately there isn't a single page we can link to for our group account. This page hosts links to all of our active decks that are ready for season play. We usually have a couple of proxied decks on the back burner, ready to play casually or still in the testing phases, and those lists may or may not be shown here.

This Aminatou deck leverages her cheap cost and unique abilities to manipulate the top of your library and blink ETB value cards. Entreat either Angels or the Dead for a huge board, or find a wonky infinite combo for the win.

Current version: 1.1

Chris's Breya deck plays a few infinite combos, and it uses heavy artifact synergy to gain value while assembling the pieces. This build seems to mesh several playstyles together and provides many engines to get going.

Current version: (not yet public)

Born from that incohesive C17 precon, this Inalla build is mostly Grixis Control with some opportunities for obscene value. This is Chris's least explosive deck, as it needs to grind out the game and gain incremental advantage.

Merfolk tribal-meets-Voltron forms Eduardo's Kumena deck. This list has a big sub-theme of tap-untap shenanigans, which means he really gets going when Paradox Engine hits the board.

Big, big mana makes Omnath a big, big boy. Eduardo's mono-green deck wants to generate enormous amounts of mana so he can smack you in the face, then sink it into a value engine. With untappers, this deck can find infinite mana in several ways.

Current version: 1.0

The Ur-Dragon might be, you know, the Ur-Dragon, but can he tutor for your win conditions? I didn't think so. Eduardo gutted his C17 deck to make this combo-Dragon build.
